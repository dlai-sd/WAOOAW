# CP My Agents Objective

## Objective

Redesign the CP My Agents experience so a newly hired agent feels like it is being brought to life, not dropped into a dense operations form. The interface should guide the customer through a clear activation journey with visible progress, emotional reassurance, and the ability to jump directly to the task they care about without losing context.

## Experience Principles

- My Agents should distinguish between `activation` and `operations` states.
- A newly hired agent should first enter an activation studio, not a general-purpose settings panel.
- Activation should progress through clear customer-facing stages: identity and voice, channel connection and validation, and operating plan.
- Each stage should explain why it matters, what is already complete, and what the next confident action is.
- Customers must be able to jump between stages or open advanced controls when they want more control, without breaking the guided flow.

## Target Stage Model

1. `Identity and Voice`
   Define the agent name, avatar or theme, and the business-facing presence the customer wants.

2. `Channel Connection`
   Connect and validate YouTube or the relevant external platform so the customer trusts that activation is real.

3. `Operating Plan`
   Confirm posting rhythm, themes, approval expectations, and other strategy controls that shape how the agent behaves.

4. `Review and Launch`
   Make the activation moment explicit so customers understand when the agent is ready to operate.

## Product Outcome

When a customer opens My Agents after hire, they should feel oriented, empowered, and safe. The screen should answer three questions immediately: `What stage am I in?`, `What should I do next?`, and `Can I jump directly to the part I care about?`
